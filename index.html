Understanding Earth's Orbital Environment

Visual Analytics 5122 | Gaurav Bharatkumar Patel

The Problem

Space debris—from defunct satellites to collision fragments—grows each year. Traveling at thousands of miles per hour, small pieces can destroy active satellites, endanger missions and astronauts, and risk cascading collisions.

Our Goal

Make this silent threat understandable and actionable through clear visuals. This project turns catalog data into an accessible narrative that summarizes accumulation, distribution, and trends in space debris.

1. Growth Timeline
Historical accumulation: From Sputnik (1957) to today. From 2 objects to 30,000+ in under 70 years.
2. Orbit Regime Dominance
Ownership by altitude: Different altitudes have different risks, traffic patterns, and lifetimes. CIS/US/China control different orbital zones.
3. Launches vs Decays
Ecosystem balance: We're adding objects faster than they decay, increasing collision risk.
4. 3D Orbital Distribution
Spatial reality: Interactive 3D view of Earth's orbital shell showing density and structure.
5. Public vs Private
Sector analysis: Growing private sector footprint across orbital regimes.
6. Altitude Pyramid
Population structure: Active vs decayed by altitude band showing altitude-specific patterns.
7. Eccentricity Map
Orbital shapes: Perigee vs apogee reveals orbital diversity affecting collision risk.
